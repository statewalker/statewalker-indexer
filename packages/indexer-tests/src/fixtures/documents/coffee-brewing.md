# Coffee Brewing Methods

## Pour Over

Pour over brewing involves slowly pouring hot water over ground coffee in a filter. Water temperature should be between 195-205°F for optimal extraction. The bloom phase, where hot water first contacts grounds, releases trapped carbon dioxide and improves flavor.

## Espresso

Espresso forces hot water through finely ground coffee at 9 bars of pressure. A proper shot takes 25-30 seconds to extract approximately 30ml of liquid. Crema, the golden foam on top, indicates proper extraction and fresh beans.

## French Press

French press steeps coarsely ground coffee in hot water for 4 minutes before pressing a metal filter down. This full-immersion method produces a rich, full-bodied cup with more oils than filtered methods. Grind consistency is critical to avoid over-extraction of fine particles.
