# Nutrition Guide

## Macronutrients

Proteins are essential for muscle repair and immune function, found in meat, fish, legumes, and dairy. Carbohydrates provide the body's primary energy source through glucose. Fats are crucial for hormone production and vitamin absorption, with unsaturated fats being the healthiest option.

## Vitamins and Minerals

Vitamin D supports bone health and immune function, synthesized through sun exposure. Iron is essential for oxygen transport in blood, with deficiency causing anemia. Calcium builds strong bones and teeth, found abundantly in dairy products and leafy greens.

## Hydration

Adults should consume approximately 2 liters of water daily, more during exercise. Dehydration impairs cognitive function and physical performance. Electrolytes like sodium and potassium must be replaced during prolonged physical activity.
