# The Solar System

## Inner Planets

Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars are the four terrestrial planets closest to the Sun. They have solid rocky surfaces and relatively thin atmospheres. Earth is unique among them for having liquid water and supporting life.

## Gas Giants

Jupiter and Saturn are massive gas giants composed primarily of hydrogen and helium. Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a storm larger than Earth that has persisted for centuries. Saturn's rings are made of ice particles ranging from tiny grains to house-sized chunks.

## Dwarf Planets

Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006. Other notable dwarf planets include Eris, Haumea, and Makemake. The Kuiper Belt beyond Neptune contains thousands of icy bodies and is the source of many short-period comets.
