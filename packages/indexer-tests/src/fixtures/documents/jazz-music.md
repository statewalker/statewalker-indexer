# Jazz Music

## Origins

Jazz originated in New Orleans in the early 20th century, blending African rhythmic traditions with European harmonic structures. Blues and ragtime were its primary precursors. The music spread north during the Great Migration as musicians moved to cities like Chicago and New York.

## Key Styles

Bebop emerged in the 1940s with complex harmonies and fast tempos, pioneered by Charlie Parker and Dizzy Gillespie. Cool jazz offered a more relaxed alternative, championed by Miles Davis and Dave Brubeck. Free jazz broke conventional rules entirely, with Ornette Coleman leading the movement.

## Improvisation

Improvisation is the heart of jazz performance. Musicians create spontaneous melodies over chord progressions called changes. Scales, arpeggios, and melodic patterns form the vocabulary that players draw from during solos.
