# Chess Strategy

## Opening Principles

Control the center with pawns and develop knights and bishops early. Castle your king to safety within the first ten moves. Avoid moving the same piece twice in the opening unless necessary to avoid material loss.

## Middlegame Tactics

Forks attack two pieces simultaneously with one piece, forcing the opponent to lose material. Pins restrict piece movement by threatening a more valuable piece behind. Discovered attacks reveal an attack when a piece moves out of the way.

## Endgame Techniques

King and pawn endgames require understanding the concept of opposition. The king must be active in the endgame, unlike the opening where it hides behind pawns. Rook endgames are the most common and require knowledge of the Lucena and Philidor positions.
