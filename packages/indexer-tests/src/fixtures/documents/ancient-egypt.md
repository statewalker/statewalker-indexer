# Ancient Egypt

## The Nile River

The Nile River was the lifeblood of ancient Egyptian civilization. Its annual flooding deposited rich silt along the riverbanks, creating fertile land for agriculture. The Egyptians developed sophisticated irrigation systems to manage water distribution.

## Pyramids and Tombs

The Great Pyramid of Giza, built for Pharaoh Khufu around 2560 BC, is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. It was constructed using approximately 2.3 million stone blocks, each weighing an average of 2.5 tons.

## Hieroglyphics

Egyptian hieroglyphics were a formal writing system combining logographic and alphabetic elements. The Rosetta Stone, discovered in 1799, provided the key to deciphering this ancient script through its parallel texts in Greek, Demotic, and hieroglyphic writing.
