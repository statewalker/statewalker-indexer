# Renewable Energy

## Solar Power

Photovoltaic cells convert sunlight directly into electricity using semiconductor materials. Solar panel efficiency has improved from 6% in the 1950s to over 25% today. Battery storage systems allow solar energy to be used after sunset, addressing intermittency concerns.

## Wind Energy

Modern wind turbines can generate up to 15 megawatts of power per unit. Offshore wind farms benefit from stronger and more consistent winds than onshore installations. Turbine blade recycling remains an environmental challenge as older installations are decommissioned.

## Hydroelectric Power

Hydroelectric dams convert the kinetic energy of flowing water into electricity. Pumped-storage hydroelectricity acts as a giant battery, pumping water uphill during low demand and releasing it during peak hours. Run-of-river systems generate power without large reservoirs.
