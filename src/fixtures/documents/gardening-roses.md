# Growing Beautiful Roses

## Choosing the Right Variety

Hybrid tea roses offer classic long-stemmed blooms. Floribunda roses
produce clusters of flowers for continuous color. Climbing roses cover
walls and trellises. David Austin English roses combine old-fashioned
form with modern disease resistance.

## Soil Preparation

Roses thrive in well-drained, slightly acidic soil (pH 6.0-6.5).
Amend heavy clay with compost and perlite. Dig a hole twice the width
of the root ball. Mix in bone meal and aged manure before planting.

## Watering and Feeding

Water deeply twice a week rather than shallow daily watering. Drip
irrigation keeps foliage dry, reducing fungal disease. Feed with
rose-specific fertilizer every 4-6 weeks during the growing season.
Stop fertilizing 6 weeks before the first expected frost.

## Pruning

Prune in late winter when forsythia blooms. Remove dead, damaged,
and crossing branches. Cut at a 45-degree angle just above an
outward-facing bud. For hybrid teas, prune to 3-5 strong canes.
