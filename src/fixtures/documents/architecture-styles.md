# Architectural Styles

## Gothic Architecture

Gothic architecture emerged in 12th-century France with pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses. These innovations allowed walls to be thinner and filled with large stained glass windows. Notre-Dame de Paris and Chartres Cathedral are iconic examples of the style.

## Art Deco

Art Deco flourished in the 1920s and 1930s, combining geometric shapes with lavish ornamentation. The Chrysler Building in New York exemplifies its bold vertical lines and metallic surfaces. The style influenced everything from skyscrapers to furniture and jewelry design.

## Brutalism

Brutalist architecture features raw concrete surfaces and massive geometric forms. Popular from the 1950s through the 1970s, it was used extensively for government buildings and universities. The style has experienced a revival of appreciation in recent years despite its controversial aesthetic.
