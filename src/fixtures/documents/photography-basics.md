# Photography Basics

## Exposure Triangle

The three fundamental settings controlling exposure are aperture, shutter speed, and ISO. Aperture controls depth of field and is measured in f-stops. Shutter speed determines motion blur, while ISO adjusts sensor sensitivity to light.

## Composition

The rule of thirds divides the frame into a 3x3 grid and places subjects at intersection points. Leading lines draw the viewer's eye through the image. Negative space around a subject creates breathing room and emphasizes the focal point.

## Post-Processing

RAW files contain unprocessed sensor data and offer maximum editing flexibility. Adjusting white balance, exposure, and contrast are common first steps. Dodging and burning selectively lighten or darken areas to guide the viewer's attention.
