# Climate Change Overview

## Causes

Human activities release greenhouse gases — primarily carbon dioxide
from burning fossil fuels, methane from agriculture and landfills,
and nitrous oxide from fertilizers. Deforestation reduces carbon
absorption. Since 1850, global temperatures have risen approximately
1.1 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels.

## Effects

Rising temperatures cause glacial retreat, sea level rise, and more
frequent extreme weather events. Ocean acidification threatens marine
ecosystems. Shifting climate zones affect agriculture, biodiversity,
and human migration patterns. Permafrost thaw releases stored methane,
creating a dangerous feedback loop.

## Mitigation

Transition to renewable energy (solar, wind, nuclear) is essential.
Carbon capture and storage technologies remove CO2 from the atmosphere.
Reforestation and regenerative agriculture sequester carbon in soil.
International agreements like the Paris Accord set emission reduction
targets for participating nations.

## Individual Actions

Reduce energy consumption, choose public transit or electric vehicles,
eat less meat (especially beef), minimize air travel, and support
policies addressing climate change. Small changes multiply across
billions of people.
